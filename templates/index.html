1. Why Fit Three Algorithms?

To guarantee structural validity and eliminate bias, we test three distinct mathematical architectures. Logistic Regression serves as a linear check, while Decision Trees provide baseline rule hierarchies. Random Forest (Champion) synthesizes 100 decorrelated decision trees, which significantly reduces variance, prevents overfitting, and successfully navigates complex academic indicators.

2. Why Use an 80/20 Split Set?

We split our 500-sample empirical student dataset into an 80% training set (400 profiles) and a 20% validation set (100 profiles). By shielding the testing set entirely during training, we force the models to prove their predictive accuracy on 100 unseen student profiles, ensuring our analytics generalize reliably to future semesters.

3. Why Recall & F1-Score Rule?

For institutional safety, missing an "At-Risk" student (False Negative) is highly critical. Raw Accuracy can be deceivingly high. Recall (Sensitivity) measures our ability to catch every single failing student, while the F1-Score is the harmonic mean balancing this sensitivity with precision. The Random Forest dominates here, maximizing proactive intervention security!

* Note: Precision measures the percentage of correct predictions among positive designations (avoiding false alarms). Recall measures the percentage of actual cases correctly flagged (avoiding missed alerts). Both parameters are balanced symmetrically with maximum stability.

Dynamic Scaling & Resilience

Design Spec: What if we had 200 samples?

Our current Augustine University cohort contains 500 validated surveys. But what if we only had 200 samples?

1. Stratified Bootstrap Booster

If sample size drops to 200, the system automatically triggers a Stratified Bootstrap Booster, reducing variance and maintaining model generalizability.

2. Feature Distillation Control

The model dynamically isolates high-yield predictors (GPA, Carryovers, Attendance) to guarantee >90% sensitivity with lower sample quantities.
